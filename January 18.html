  2. Three teams vie for AMS executive

    Teams BGP, PDA and TNL officially launched their executive campaigns at midnight

    BY HOLLY TOUSIGNANT NEWS EDITOR



    Left to Right: Eril Berkok, CompSci ’12, Alexander Prescott, ArtSci ’14, and Troy Sherman, ArtSci ’14, the AMS presidential candidates.

    Candidates for this year’s AMS executive election were ratified last night at AMS Assembly, with three teams in the running.

    Team BGP members include presidential candidate Eril Berkok, CompSci ’12, vice-president of operations candidate Peter Green, ArtSci ’13, and vice-president of university affairs candidate TK Pritchard, ArtSci ’12.

    Team PDA consists of Alexander Prescott, ArtSci ’14, who is running for president, vice-president of operations candidate Craig Draeger, ArtSci ’13, and vice-president of university affairs candidate Lisa Acchione, ArtSci ’14.

    Team TNL is made up of presidential candidate Troy Sherman, ArtSci ’14, vice-president of operations candidate Nicola Plummer, Comm ’13, and vice-president of university affairs candidate Liam Faught, ArtSci ’14.

    The campaign period officially began at midnight and will continue until Jan. 28. The election dates are Jan. 29 and 30. For full AMS and faculty election coverage, follow the Journal and queensjournal.ca in the upcoming weeks.

    Why is your team running? We believe that our collective and diverse experiences will enable us to govern the AMS in a way that works for the student body. In our time at Queen’s, we’ve recognized that there are a number of both student demographics and Faculty Societies that feel disenfranchised at our university. In order for the AMS to truly fulfill its mission statement to serve and represent the diversity of students at Queen’s, a top-down government reform is necessary. We sincerely need a more equitable representation of these students’ interests in the AMS. This is the principle reason Team BGP is running for AMS Executive.

    What can students expect from your team during the campaign? Students can expect Team BGP to take an honest and respectful approach to our opponents. These are six individuals we are very much proud to run with so you will never see us swinging for knees. We are confident and incredibly exited to share our platform with students we feel our ideas fully speak for themselves. We feel deeply that our platform’s four pillars of Health, Wellness and Safety, New and Improved AMS Services, Academics and Employment, and Supporting Student Life offer a strong vision and carefully laid course for the AMS.

    What is one thing students would be surprised to find out about your team? Students would be surprised that our poster photos were taken in Peter's bathtub, because that's where the lighting was best at the time!

    Why is your team running? We are running to make the AMS become more representative of its membership. We understand the disenfranchisement prevailing amongst students; this comes from an internal culture that promotes objectives that are not necessarily important to the greater student membership. We want to make the AMS more relevant to students.

    What can students expect from your team during the campaign? Honesty. A comprehensive platform with points that are relevant to all members of the AMS. We will focus on affordability of your degree, mental and physical health issues and making the AMS more sustainable – to name a few.

    What is one thing students would be surprised to learn about your team? Our 20-point contract where we promise part of our salary (5%) for every promise we “break”!
